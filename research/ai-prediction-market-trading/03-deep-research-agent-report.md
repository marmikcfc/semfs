## Part 3 — Academic research: LLM forecasting performance

### 3.1 Halawi et al. 2024 — the benchmark numbers

> **"A retrieval-augmented LLM forecasting system (GPT-4-1106-Preview + fine-tuned GPT-4-0613, with news retrieval, scratchpad reasoning, and trimmed-mean ensembling) achieves Brier score .179 vs the human crowd aggregate .149 — i.e., it approaches but does not beat the competitive-forecaster crowd unconditionally (accuracy 71.5% vs 77.0%)."**

- Source: [arxiv.org/abs/2402.18563](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.18563) `primary` | Vote: **3-0 ✓**
- Quote: _"our averaged Brier score is .179, while the crowd achieves .149, resulting in a difference of .03. Our accuracy on the test set is 71.5%, whereas the community scores 77.0%"_

> **"The LLM system significantly OUTPERFORMS the human crowd specifically when the crowd is uncertain (crowd prediction between .3 and .7), scoring Brier .199 vs the crowd's .246."**

- Source: [arxiv.org/abs/2402.18563](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.18563) `primary` | Vote: **2-1 ✓**
- Quote: _"when the crowd's predictions are between .3 and .7, our Brier score is .199 compared to the crowd's .246"_
- **Strategic implication:** machine edge concentrates in the uncertainty zone (30–70% markets). These are exactly the markets where behavioral fading / disciplined contrarianism also works.

## Part 4 — Principles: the Kelly math (verified to source)

### 4.1 The exact formula

> **"The Kelly criterion prescribes a bet fraction `f* = p − q/b = p − (1−p)/b` for binary bets, where p is win probability, q=1−p, and b is net odds; the general investment form is `f* = p/l − q/g` for partial-loss outcomes."**

- Source: [en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kelly_criterion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kelly_criterion) `secondary` | Vote: **3-0 ✓**
- Quote: _"f* = p − q/b = p − (1−p)/b … f* = p/l − q/g"_

> **"The Kelly criterion maximizes the long-term expected value of the logarithm of wealth, equivalent to maximizing the long-term expected geometric growth rate of capital."**

- Source: [en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kelly_criterion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kelly_criterion) `secondary` | Vote: **3-0 ✓**

### 4.2 Why practitioners use fractional Kelly

> **"Practitioners deliberately use fractional (e.g. half) Kelly to reduce risk of ruin, reduce volatility, account for model/estimation error, and tolerate drawdowns emotionally."**

- Source: [en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kelly_criterion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kelly_criterion) `secondary` | Vote: **3-0 ✓**
- Quote: _"Gamblers would use less than full Kelly in order to reduce the chance of ruin, reduce volatility, and account for model error. Due to the high drawdowns, gamblers in practice find fractional Kellies much better emotionally than full Kelly."_
