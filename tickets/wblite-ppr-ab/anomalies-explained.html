④ Verdict (forming-opinions)

Opinion: the two anomalies are one phenomenon — plain is exceptionally strong in kaifa (cheapest) and houqin (most accurate), and retrieval can't improve on a maxed baseline — ~70%.
Load-bearing (confirmed): kaifa = lowest plain tokens; houqin = highest plain accuracy, across all 4 personas. The anomaly lands exactly where plain peaks on that axis.
Mechanism (hypothesis, not yet measured): kaifa's code dirs reward structure-navigation (cheap); houqin's docs reward breadth-browsing (accurate). Retrieval adds reads where navigation was already cheap, and misranks where the crawl already found the answer.
Strongest case against: the KG/index quality could be the real driver (over-connected code KG inflating kaifa reads; weak houqin embeddings misranking) — plain's strength would then be a coincidence, not the cause.
I'd change my mind if: per-cell plain traces show kaifa plain does not navigate by structure (reads just as much), or houqin plain does not browse-find — or if a 5th persona with cheap plain shows no PPR token rise.

⑤ So what

The anomalies are not bugs in PPR — they're plain being a very strong baseline on structured (kaifa) and browsable (houqin) workspaces. Retrieval pays off when the workspace is too big/unstructured to navigate (chanpin, yunying); it can't beat a good crawl when navigation already works. Lever: route by workspace shape — use retrieval where plain navigation fails, not as a blanket replacement.
